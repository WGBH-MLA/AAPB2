## Introduction

In 2015, Justice Anthony Kennedy issued one of the most consequential Supreme Court decisions in modern U.S. history. Kennedy’s opinion held that LGBTQ+ people merely sought to be included in “one of civilization’s oldest institutions. Far from disrespecting the institution of marriage, Kennedy argued that same-sex couples “respect it so deeply that they seek to find its fulfillment for themselves…They ask for equal dignity in the eyes of the law. The Constitution grants them that right.” The decision immediately repealed all state-level bans, and within hours, clerks across the country began to issue marriage licenses to same-sex couples. Unsurprisingly, the first episode of This Way Out (a weekly radio magazine about LGBTQ+ issues) after Obergefell was devoted almost entirely to the impact of the decision. This excerpt features public statements from President Barack Obama, lead plaintiff Jim Obergefell, and attorney Evan Wolfson (of Freedom to Marry, an organization that had spent years fighting for marriage equality). The segment also includes a statement denouncing the decision from Kellie Fiodereck, a disappointed spokesperson for the Alliance for Defending Freedom.
