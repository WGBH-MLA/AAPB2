## Introduction

People who followed the news in the 2000s might have been surprised to see conservative Ted Olson and liberal David Boies joining together on a legal challenge. Olson and Boies had represented opposite sides of the *Bush v. Gore* (2000) court case that settled the outcome of the 2000 presidential election, but the two men came together to advocate for marriage rights. During this segment of a 2010 episode of *Bill Moyers Journal*, the two attorneys defend same-sex marriage against common challenges. Were gay rights advocates “inventing” a right to marry that wasn’t in the Constitution? Were legal challenges undermining the voice of the people by seeking to undermine democratically enacted laws? Olson and Boies provide legal arguments rooted in the 14th Amendment's guarantees of equality protection, and argue it is necessary for the courts to continue playing an important role as defender of minority rights in a democratic government.
