## Introduction

When Barack Obama ran for president in 2008, he positioned himself in support of civil unions for same-sex couples, but not marriage. After suffering disappointing losses in the 2004 election, many Democrats (even those who, like Obama, had expressed support for marriage equality in the past) felt that the compromise of supporting civil unions was a more prudent political strategy. However, in May 2012, in the midst of his election campaign against Republican Mitt Romney, Obama announced in a primetime interview that his position had evolved, and he now supported marriage for same-sex couples. This PBS NewsHour piece includes excerpts from that Obama interview, as well as additional reporting and an interview with journalist Kerry Eleveld.
