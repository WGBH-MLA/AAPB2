## Introduction

In 2004, Democrats were hopeful to regain the White House and the congressional majority, but Republicans ultimately won decisive victories across the country. Many commentators noted that the issue of marriage equality seemed to have been electorally beneficial for the GOP. The report from This Way Out, a radio magazine show about gay and lesbian issues, catalogues examples of Republican politicians who used anti-gay rhetoric while campaigning, and documents numerous instances of states that passed legislation or constitutional amendments banning marriage for same-sex couples. While commentators disagreed on the role that LGBTQ+ rights debates played in explaining broader electoral outcomes, the report makes clear that election night was disappointing for many LGBTQ+ advocates (note: this report uses the term “Les-Bi-Gay” to refer to Lesbian, Bisexual, and Gay People).
