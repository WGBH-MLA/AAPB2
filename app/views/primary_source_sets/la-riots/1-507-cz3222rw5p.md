## Introduction

In the early hours of March 3, 1991, Los Angeles police officers sought to apprehend Black motorist Rodney King after a high-speed chase in which they suspected he was driving while intoxicated. The officers severely beat King and continued to pummel him long after he was on the ground. Bystander George Holliday captured the incident on his camcorder, and that footage aired on newscasts across the country in the coming weeks. This segment from The MacNeil/Lehrer NewsHour includes some of the infamous footage and also explores the response in Los Angeles—both from those like LA Police Chief Daryl Gates who framed King’s beating as an “aberration,” and from Black community members and leaders who argued that the violent incident was typical for the Los Angeles Police Department.
