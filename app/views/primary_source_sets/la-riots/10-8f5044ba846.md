## Introduction

While 96 percent of Black Americans polled by the LA Times in 1992 disagreed with the verdict acquitting the four LAPD officers in the Rodney King verdict, there were divisions within the Black community about how to understand the LA Riots and move forward. On this episode of Bill Moyers’ Listening to America series, rapper Sister Souljah expresses the sentiment of more radical activists. She characterizes America as a “white racist institution” and describes the riots as a “rebellion” carried out by young Black people who had been “looted.” Conversely, Robert Woodson, president of the National Center for Neighborhood Enterprise, argues that Black residents are “less concerned about” by “racism” than by “drug dealers” within their own community, and points to the rising number of Black children born out of wedlock. In this clip, we see a debate that has played out in the Black community throughout history: do Black people need to bond together socially and politically to challenge racist systems, or should they look inward, emphasizing personal responsibility and racial uplift?
