## Introduction

The racial dynamics of the LA Riots were unmistakable. Rodney King was a Black victim of police brutality at the hands of white police officers. The participants in the LA Riots were predominantly Black and Latino, and the unrest was concentrated in LA’s communities of color. Many stores owned by Korean proprietors were looted. Given this reality, it should not be surprising that the events stimulated discourse about race and racism in America. Many Americans felt that the riots were best understood as members of marginalized communities lashing out against decades of prejudice and discrimination under an oppressive system. However, other Americans resented the implication that white Americans, or a history of racial discrimination, were somehow responsible for the unrest. One example of this back-and-forth about race in America can be found in this special episode of Life & Times, aired on KCET in Los Angeles, entitled “Young LA: Rage & Responsibility.” This forum of young LA residents, from a range of class and racial backgrounds, aired just a few weeks after the King verdict. The program features some of this debate and gives a sense of the emotional intensity of the moment.
