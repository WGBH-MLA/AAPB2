## Introduction

The day after election day, the question of who would be the next president of the United States remained unresolved. This episode of The NewsHour with Jim Lehrer features both George W. Bush and Al Gore addressing their respective supporters with optimism about claiming the presidency. Their speeches also aimed to reassure the American people amid the uncertainty, encouraging voters to have patience, faith, and confidence in the rule of law in electoral processes.This message would become critical during the byzantine processes of recounts, lawsuits, state court orders, and the Supreme Court’s ruling, as there would be no officially named president until December: 36 days after the election.
