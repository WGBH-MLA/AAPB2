## Introduction

While progressives expressed frustrations and concerns throughout the Great Recession, the most impactful outpouring of popular anger came in 2011 in the form of the Occupy Wall Street movement. The spark came on September 17, when a group of protesters took over Zuccotti Park, a privately-owned but publicly accessible park in the heart of New York City’s financial district. Crucially, the protesters brought tents and refused to leave; in the coming weeks, the size of the encampment grew as protesters traveled from across the country and a small community formed in Zuccotti. This *PBS NewsHour* report from October 5, 2011, featuring interviews, footage and reporting from economics correspondent Paul Solman, provides a valuable snapshot of the Occupy Wall Street movement, giving insights into the ideological perspectives and tactics of the protesters.
