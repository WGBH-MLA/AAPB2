# PowerPoint

## Thumbnail

![PowerPoint](https://s3.amazonaws.com/americanarchive.org/special-collections/powerpoint-thumbnail.png "PowerPoint")

## Summary

The *PowerPoint* special collection comprises nearly 200 episodes of the nation’s first live call-in program dedicated to issues of concern to African American listeners. The collection spans from 1997 through 2006, documenting an era of profound social and political change for Black Americans.

The program engaged African American listeners from all walks of life and at every stage of life, with episodes focused on issues impacting men, women, youth, and the elderly. Notable episodes explored topics such as [Black fatherhood](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-ebead81c513) and [men’s health](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-ae3244973dc); [motherhood](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-b57f708cf84), [menopause](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-cde904bb3b4), and [heart disease among African American women](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-a6620462d56); [teen pregnancy](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-2c60121cea3) and [juvenile justice](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-d05b0d0f420); and senior-focused discussions of [Alzheimer’s disease](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-bd0d04c2c02) and [Medicare](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-f4c6ea55274).

A hallmark of *PowerPoint* was its candid discussion of race. The series’ [premiere episode](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-30443c67148) answered then-President Bill Clinton’s call for an honest dialogue on race with a conversation featuring history professor Roger Wilkins and poet Nikki Giovanni. This commitment to addressing race and racism, both in the United States and globally, continued in future episodes. Each February, PowerPoint analyzed the National Urban League's annual *State of Black America* report, while other episodes confronted systemic racism in the form of [racial profiling](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-2126bf72748), [police brutality](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-3478065d80a), and the use of [deadly force](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-c5b003d0157) by law enforcement against people of color. *PowerPoint* also aired episodes on [racial healing](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-a8ed71f2040) and [racial reconciliation](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-0660ffa337d).

*PowerPoint* covered major political events of the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries, from the [impeachment of Bill Clinton](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-05c923a192b) to the post-9/11 [War on Terror](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-18dca9d2475). Many episodes reflected the series’ international focus. For example, the “Ambassadors' Roundtable” subseries featured diplomats like Jordan’s Marwan Muasher discussing the [Persian Gulf crisis](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-1c0028dfe6a) and Sierra Leone’s John Leigh addressing [human rights atrocities](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-1c03762091a) in his country. 

The series often featured individuals working at the cross section of art and activism, such as [Dick Gregory](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-e71e8dba6b8), [Ossie Davis and Ruby Dee](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-1b44c7b1d9d), and the iconic Nina Simone. Other notable guests included civil rights theorist [Lani Guinier](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-7e6d9fd85b7), science fiction luminary [Octavia E. Butler](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-c83e6e7bd79), and baseball legend Buck O’Neil. Public figures from across the political spectrum also appeared on *PowerPoint*, such as neurosurgeon and future Republican presidential candidate [Dr. Benjamin Carson](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-25ec7fbbc64), prominent [Black Republicans](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-2126bf72748), and San Francisco Democratic Mayor [Willie Brown](https://americanarchive.org/catalog/cpb-aacip-c697effdc02). 

## Background

*PowerPoint* was the first national call-in radio program to focus on issues affecting African Americans and the first African American news and information talk show to air on public radio nationwide. Created and produced by executive producer Reggie Hicks, the Atlanta-based show aired from 9:00 PM to 11:00 PM on Sunday evenings for seven years, reaching listeners in fifty media markets on NPR and Sirius satellite radio (now SiriusXM). 

Episodes of *PowerPoint* were contributed to the AAPB by the University of Maryland Libraries and preserved through a grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. The *PowerPoint* special collection launched in February 2026.
